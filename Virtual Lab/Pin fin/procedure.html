Test Procedure to Study the temperature distribution along the length of pin fin in natural & forced convection

AIM:To determine temperature distribution and fin efficiency of a pin fin in natural and forced convection.

APPARATUS:
The apparatus consists of a metal pipe with two layers of insulation. An electric heating coil wound on a silica rod is placed at the center. The ends are thickly insulated to prevent heat loss so that heat flows only in a radial direction. Three thermocouples each are placed at different radii to measure the temperature distribution within the cylinder.

SPECIFICATIONS:
1. Duct size = 15 cm x 10 cm
2. Diameter of the pin fin = 1.27 cm
3. Diameter of the orifice = 2 cm
4. Diameter of the delivery pipe = (Int.) = 42 mm
5. Coefficient of discharg (orifice meter) Cd = 0.64
6. Centrifugal blower 1 H.P single phase motor.
7. No. of thermocouples on fin = 5; (1) to (5) as shown in and indicated on temperature indicator.
8. Thermocouple 6 reads ambient temperature in side the duct.
9. Thermal conductivity of fin material (brass) = 110 W/m.K(approx.)
10. Temperature Indicator 0 - 3000C with compensation of ambient temperature up to 50 0c.

PROCEDURE:
Natural convection:
1. The pin is heated by using heater and adjusting the voltage to 80 V by dimmerstat.
2. The temperature at various points are noted including ambient temperatures.
3. After steady state is reached, the final readings of the thermocouples (1 to 5) in addition to the ambient temp are recorded.
4. The experiment is repeated by varying voltage say 100, 120.
Forced convection:
1. The fin is heated by using the heater and required voltage is adjusted by using dimmer-stat.
2. The blower is started by using the switch.With the help of valve the manometer is adjusted to required height.
3. The temperatures are recorded for 1 to 5 points for an interval of 5 minutes.
4. The final readings are noted after steady state is reached.
5. The experiment is repeated for different manometer heights.

CALCULATIONS:

(A) Calculation of value of h in natural convection:
1.The average temperature of the fin Ts =(T1+T2+T3+T4+T5)/5 = ℃
Air mean temperature Tf =(Ts+T6)/2 = ℃
The properties of air µ, Cp, Kair, and Pr are taken from steam tables at Tf
2. Determine (Gr×Pr)
Gr = Grashof Number = g*β*ΔT*(L^3)/(V^2)
Pr = Prandtl number = (μ*Cp)/Kair
3. The empirical relations of air for free convection are
a. Nu = 1.1*(Gr.Pr)^1.7 for 0.1 (<) Gr.pr (<) 10^4
b. Nu = 0.59*(Gr.Pr)^0.25 for 10^4(<)Gr.Pr (<) 10^9
c. Nu = 0.13*(Gr.Pr)^(1/3) for 10^9 (<)Gr.Pr (<) 10^12
4. Determine heat transfer coefficient
Nu = Nusset number =(h*Lc)/Kair Where Lc = d
5. The heat transfer from a fin
Q= ((hpKA)^0.5)*(Ts-T∞)*tanhmL Watt
6. Determine fin efficiency
ηfin= (tanhmL)/(mL), where m= (hP/KA)^0.5 , where P=πd , A = (π*d*d)/4
(B) Calculation of h in forced convection:
1. The discharge of air through orifice
(Qa ) = Cd*A*((2gha)^0.5)
Where, Cd = Coefficient of discharge of the orifice (0.64)
A = Area of cross-section of orifice = (π*d*d)/4
d = Diameter of the orifice in meters, hw = h1- h2 /100 m of water
ha = ρwhw/ρa ,where ρw = density of water = 1000 kg/m^3, ρa = density of air = 1.2 kg/m^3
The velocity of air across the fins
u=(Qa/duct area)*((Tf+273)/(Ta+273)) (use this velocity in calculation of Renolds number)
2. Determine the heat transfer coefficient using the below relations
(a) Nu = 0.683*(Re)^0.466 when 40(<)Re (<) 4000
b) Nu= 0.174*(Re)^0.618 when 4000 (<) Re (<) 40000
Where Nu =(h*d)/Kair , Re = ρ*u*d/μ (v=µ/ρ)
The properties of air µ, Cp, Kair, , ρ, and Pr are taken from steam tables at mean air temperature (Tf)
